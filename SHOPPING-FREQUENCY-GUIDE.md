## Choose Your Shopping Frequency

### Quick Decision Guide

| Factor | 1x Per Week | 2x Per Week | 3x Per Week |
|--------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Time Commitment** | 1-2 hours | 2-3 hours total | 3-4 hours total |
| **Produce Freshness** | Good | Great | Excellent |
| **Shelf-Stable Hauls** | Large | Medium | Medium |
| **Best For** | Busy schedules, good meal prep | Balanced approach | Fresh produce lovers |
| **Freezer Space Needed** | Moderate-High | Moderate | Low |

## 1x Per Week Shopping Strategy

**📅 Shop: Sunday  
⏱️ Time: 90-120 minutes  
🎯 Coverage: All 7 days (Monday-Sunday)**

### What to Buy on Sunday

#### All Shelf-Stable Items (Buy for entire week)
These items last 2+ weeks in the pantry:
- [ ] All oils (ghee, coconut oil, avocado oil, olive oil)
- [ ] All nuts and nut butters
- [ ] All seasonings and spices
- [ ] Bone broth (shelf-stable or freezer)
- [ ] Canned tomatoes and compliant sauces
- [ ] Honey (for cooking)
- [ ] Coconut products
- [ ] Whole grains (if planning to add after Whole30)
- [ ] Salt and pepper

#### Fresh Proteins (4-7 days shelf life)
- [ ] Chicken breasts (2-3 lbs)
- [ ] Ground beef (2-3 lbs)
- [ ] Salmon or other fish (1-2 lbs)
- [ ] Turkey sausage (1 lb)
- [ ] Eggs (2-3 dozen)
- [ ] Pork or lamb (optional)

#### Hardy Vegetables (Last 5-7 days)
- [ ] Sweet potatoes (5-6)
- [ ] Carrots (2 lbs)
- [ ] Broccoli (3-4 crowns)
- [ ] Cauliflower (2 heads)
- [ ] Bell peppers (4-5, any color)
- [ ] Onions (3-4)
- [ ] Celery (1 bunch)
- [ ] Brussels sprouts (1-2 lbs)
- [ ] Garlic (1 bulb)

#### Delicate Vegetables (3-5 days shelf life)
- [ ] Spinach or kale (pre-packed or fresh)
- [ ] Zucchini (2-3)
- [ ] Mushrooms (1 lb)
- [ ] Asparagus (1 bunch)
- [ ] Green beans (1 lb)
- [ ] Tomatoes (4-5, if in season)

#### Fruits (3-5 days shelf life)
- [ ] Apples (5-6, last longer)
- [ ] Berries (2 containers)
- [ ] Bananas (3-4)
- [ ] Avocados (3-4)
- [ ] Oranges or citrus (3-4)
- [ ] Lemons/limes (3-4)

### Storage Strategy for 1x Weekly

**Immediate Use (Days 1-3):**
- Store delicate vegetables on top shelf with paper towels
- Keep berries in original container
- Store avocados on counter, move to fridge when ripe

**Mid-Week Use (Days 4-7):**
- Keep hardy vegetables in crisper drawer
- Consider freezing extra salmon or ground beef on Day 2
- Use frozen vegetables on Days 5-7 for variety

**Pro Tip:** On Day 2-3, portion and freeze half your protein for Days 5-7!

## 2x Per Week Shopping Strategy

**📅 Shop: Sunday & Wednesday  
⏱️ Time: 1.5 hours (Sunday) + 30 min (Wednesday)  
🎯 Coverage: Days split into 4-3 day periods**

### Shopping Trip #1: Sunday

**Goal:** Buy ALL shelf-stable items for the entire week + fresh items for Days 1-4

#### All Shelf-Stable Items (Full Week Supply)
- [ ] All oils and cooking fats
- [ ] All nuts and nut butters
- [ ] All seasonings and spices
- [ ] Bone broth
- [ ] Canned tomatoes and sauces
- [ ] Honey, sweeteners
- [ ] Coconut products
- [ ] Salt, pepper, basic pantry

**Why all at once?** You only want to shop the bulk pantry items once. This makes Wednesday a quick trip!

#### Fresh Proteins (Days 1-4: Monday-Thursday)
- [ ] Chicken breasts (2 lbs)
- [ ] Ground beef (1.5-2 lbs)
- [ ] Eggs (2 dozen)
- [ ] Salmon or fish (1 lb, optional)

#### Vegetables for Days 1-4 (Choose hardy ones)
- [ ] Sweet potatoes (3-4)
- [ ] Carrots (2 lbs)
- [ ] Broccoli (2 crowns)
- [ ] Cauliflower (1 head)
- [ ] Bell peppers (3-4)
- [ ] Onions (2-3)
- [ ] Garlic (1 bulb)
- [ ] Spinach (1 container)
- [ ] Mushrooms (½ lb)

#### Fruits for Days 1-4
- [ ] Apples (4-5)
- [ ] Berries (1-2 containers)
- [ ] Avocados (2-3)
- [ ] Bananas (2-3)
- [ ] Citrus (2-3)

### Shopping Trip #2: Wednesday (Mid-Week Refresh)

**Goal:** Buy FRESH items for Days 5-7 (Friday-Sunday) - NO pantry items needed!

**Expected time:** 30-40 minutes (much shorter than Sunday!)

#### Fresh Proteins Only (Days 5-7: Friday-Sunday)
- [ ] Chicken (1.5 lbs, or leftover from Sunday if frozen)
- [ ] Ground beef or pork (1-1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Eggs (1 dozen, or use leftover)
- [ ] Fish or shrimp (optional)

#### Fresh Vegetables (Days 5-7)
Choose the crispest, freshest options:
- [ ] Leafy greens (spinach, kale, mixed greens)
- [ ] Zucchini (1-2)
- [ ] Asparagus (½ bunch)
- [ ] Green beans (½ lb)
- [ ] Broccoli or cauliflower (1 crown)
- [ ] Bell peppers (2-3)
- [ ] Tomatoes (if in season)
- [ ] Sweet potatoes (1-2)
- [ ] Carrots (½ lb, or use leftover)

#### Fresh Fruits (Days 5-7)
- [ ] Berries (1-2 containers)
- [ ] Apples (2-3, or use leftover)
- [ ] Avocados (1-2)
- [ ] Bananas (1-2)
- [ ] Citrus (1-2)

### 2x Weekly Benefit

✅ **Sunday = Big haul (pantry + 4 days fresh)**  
✅ **Wednesday = Quick refresh (fresh only)**  
✅ **Peak freshness:** Vegetables on Wednesday are as crisp as the day they were picked  
✅ **Flexibility:** Can easily swap meals if you find better produce mid-week

## 3x Per Week Shopping Strategy

**📅 Shop: Sunday, Wednesday & Friday  
⏱️ Time: 1-1.5 hours each trip  
🎯 Coverage: Days split into 3-2-2 day periods**

### Shopping Trip #1: Sunday

**Goal:** Buy ALL shelf-stable items for entire week + fresh items for Days 1-3

#### All Shelf-Stable Items (Full Week Supply)
- [ ] All oils and cooking fats
- [ ] All nuts and nut butters
- [ ] All seasonings and spices
- [ ] Bone broth
- [ ] Canned goods
- [ ] Pantry staples
- [ ] Honey, sweeteners

#### Fresh Proteins (Days 1-3: Monday-Wednesday)
- [ ] Chicken (1.5-2 lbs)
- [ ] Ground beef or pork (1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Eggs (1-2 dozen)
- [ ] Fish (optional, 1 lb)

#### Fresh Vegetables (Days 1-3)
- [ ] Sweet potatoes (2-3)
- [ ] Carrots (1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Broccoli (2 crowns)
- [ ] Bell peppers (2-3)
- [ ] Onions (2)
- [ ] Garlic
- [ ] Spinach or mixed greens (1 container)
- [ ] Zucchini (1-2)
- [ ] Mushrooms (½ lb)

#### Fresh Fruits (Days 1-3)
- [ ] Apples (3-4)
- [ ] Berries (1 container)
- [ ] Avocados (2)
- [ ] Bananas (2)
- [ ] Citrus (2-3)

### Shopping Trip #2: Wednesday (Mid-Week Refresh)

**Goal:** Buy FRESH items for Days 4-5 (Thursday-Friday) - NO pantry items

#### Fresh Proteins (Days 4-5)
- [ ] Chicken (1-1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Ground beef or different protein (1-1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Eggs (1 dozen, if needed)

#### Fresh Vegetables (Days 4-5)
- [ ] Leafy greens (spinach, kale, mixed)
- [ ] Zucchini or cucumber
- [ ] Asparagus
- [ ] Broccoli or cauliflower
- [ ] Bell peppers (1-2)
- [ ] Cherry tomatoes (if in season)
- [ ] Sweet potatoes (1-2)

#### Fresh Fruits (Days 4-5)
- [ ] Berries
- [ ] Apples or other fruit in season
- [ ] Avocados (1-2)
- [ ] Citrus

### Shopping Trip #3: Friday (Weekend Refresh)

**Goal:** Buy FRESH items for Days 6-7 (Saturday-Sunday) - NO pantry items

#### Fresh Proteins (Days 6-7)
- [ ] Chicken (1 lb)
- [ ] Ground beef, pork, or fish (1-1.5 lbs)
- [ ] Eggs (1 dozen, if needed)

#### Fresh Vegetables (Days 6-7)
- [ ] Leafy greens
- [ ] Vegetables for your Saturday & Sunday meals
- [ ] Any premium items you want extra fresh for weekend

#### Fresh Fruits (Days 6-7)
- [ ] Berries or other favorites
- [ ] Apples
- [ ] Avocados

### 3x Weekly Benefit

✅ **Maximum freshness:** Every item purchased is at peak ripeness  
✅ **Flexibility:** Adjust meals based on what's best each visit  
✅ **Less waste:** Produce doesn't sit around getting old  
✅ **Best for:** Those who love fresh ingredients and enjoy frequent trips  
❌ **Trade-off:** More time spent shopping, but less food waste

## Which Strategy is Right for You?

### Choose 1x Per Week If:
- ✅ You have a busy schedule and want fewer store trips
- ✅ You're comfortable with meal prep and planning ahead
- ✅ You don't mind some produce being older by end of week
- ✅ You have good freezer space
- ✅ You want to batch cook most meals on Sunday

**Example:** Busy professional, coach, parent with hectic week

### Choose 2x Per Week If:
- ✅ You want a balance between convenience and freshness
- ✅ You like flexibility to adjust meals mid-week
- ✅ You have moderate meal prep time
- ✅ You prefer moderately fresh produce
- ✅ You want the best of both worlds

**Example:** Most people find this is the sweet spot!

### Choose 3x Per Week If:
- ✅ You love the freshest possible ingredients
- ✅ You enjoy frequent shopping trips
- ✅ You want to minimize food waste
- ✅ You're flexible with your weekly meal plan
- ✅ You live near a great grocery store

**Example:** Food enthusiasts, those with flexible schedules, farmers market lovers

## Shopping List Organization

### For 1x Weekly Shoppers

Print the `shopping-list.md` template once and fill it completely for all 7 days.

**Organization by aisle:**
1. Produce first (all veggies & fruits)
2. Meat counter (all proteins)
3. Eggs
4. Pantry (oils, spices, shelf-stable items)
5. Frozen section (if adding frozen vegetables)

### For 2x Weekly Shoppers

**Sunday Shopping List:**
- [ ] Print `shopping-list.md` and fill for "Days 1-4 (4-day supply)"
- [ ] Include FULL pantry/spice section
- [ ] Include hardy vegetables only

**Wednesday Shopping List:**
- [ ] Print `shopping-list.md` again and fill for "Days 5-7 (3-day supply)"
- [ ] **SKIP pantry section** (you already bought Sunday)
- [ ] Focus on fresh produce and proteins only

**Tip:** Create a template note on your phone that lists what to skip on Wednesday!

### For 3x Weekly Shoppers

**Sunday Shopping List:**
- [ ] Days 1-3 (3-day supply)
- [ ] Include FULL pantry section
- [ ] Hardy vegetables

**Wednesday Shopping List:**
- [ ] Days 4-5 (2-day supply)
- [ ] **SKIP pantry**
- [ ] Fresh vegetables and proteins only

**Friday Shopping List:**
- [ ] Days 6-7 (2-day supply)
- [ ] **SKIP pantry**
- [ ] Premium fresh items for weekend meals

## Pro Tips by Frequency

### 1x Weekly Tips

- **Freeze on Day 2:** Portion half your proteins into freezer bags with labels
- **Rotate vegetables:** Use hardy ones Days 1-4, frozen/stored Days 5-7
- **Buy sale items:** Meat and salmon often go on sale - buy extra and freeze
- **Pre-chop:** Spend 30 minutes chopping all vegetables right after shopping
- **Batch cook:** Make large portions Sunday that reheat well all week

### 2x Weekly Tips

- **Sunday is big:** Allot 90 minutes and bring your main shopping list
- **Wednesday is quick:** 30 minutes max - just fresh items
- **Plan your meals:** Know exactly which recipes you're making Days 1-4
- **Buy seasonal:** Wednesday is perfect for picking seasonal items at their peak
- **Save receipts:** Track spending by comparing Sunday vs. Wednesday trips

### 3x Weekly Tips

- **Bring good bags:** Three smaller trips means easier carrying
- **Shop near home:** Pick a grocery store close by for quick visits
- **Know your layout:** Learn where everything is for speedy shopping
- **Buy strategically:** Proteins on sale? Grab extra for other days
- **Farmers market option:** Use one of your trips for the farmers market for ultra-fresh produce

## Money-Saving Tips by Frequency

### 1x Weekly Shopping
- Buy in bulk (better deals on larger packages)
- Use sales and stock up on shelf-stable items
- Buy whole chickens instead of breasts (cheaper per lb)

### 2x Weekly Shopping
- Sunday deals are usually good
- Wednesday often has mid-week produce specials
- Split budget to balance cash flow

### 3x Weekly Shopping
- More flexibility to take advantage of daily deals
- Better for buying exactly what you need (less waste)
- Watch for flash sales on proteins
