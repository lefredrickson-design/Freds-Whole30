## Meal Prep Philosophy

### Why Meal Prep?
- **Saves time:** Cook once, eat multiple times
- **Ensures compliance:** Pre-made meals = no temptation to cheat
- **Reduces stress:** Know exactly what you're eating each day
- **Saves money:** Batch cooking is more economical
- **Prevents decision fatigue:** Meals are ready to go

### The Whole30 Meal Prep Method
We recommend a **"mix & match" approach** where you prep components (proteins, vegetables, healthy fats) separately and combine them throughout the week. This prevents food boredom and allows flexibility.

- [ ] Pack lunch from prepped containers

## Protein Prep

### Best Proteins for Meal Prep
1. **Chicken Breast** - Most versatile, long shelf life
2. **Ground Beef** - Great for bulk cooking
3. **Salmon** - Rich in omega-3s
4. **Pork** - Affordable and flavorful
5. **Ground Turkey** - Lean option
6. **Eggs** - Hard-boil in batches

### How to Prep: Chicken Breast

**Method 1: Oven Baked (Best for Bulk)**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F
2. Season chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and herbs
3. Place on sheet pan with ghee
4. Bake 20-25 minutes until internal temp is 165°F
5. Let cool, slice or shred
6. Store in glass containers (lasts 3-4 days)
7. **Batch size:** 6-8 breasts = 3-4 meals for one person

**Method 2: Slow Cooker (Hands-Off)**
1. Place chicken breasts in slow cooker
2. Add 1 cup bone broth + salt/pepper
3. Cook on low 4-6 hours
4. Shred with forks
5. Store in glass containers with cooking liquid
6. **Time:** 10 min prep, hands-off cooking

**Method 3: Instant Pot (Fastest)**
1. Add 1 cup water + trivet to Instant Pot
2. Place chicken on trivet
3. Season chicken
4. Pressure cook on high 8-10 minutes
5. Quick release, shred
6. **Time:** 15 minutes total

### How to Prep: Ground Beef

**Method: Sheet Pan Browning**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F
2. Place ground beef on sheet pan
3. Break into crumbles
4. Add diced onions and minced garlic
5. Bake 15-20 minutes, stirring halfway through
6. Season with salt, pepper, cumin
7. Drain excess fat (save for cooking)
8. Cool and portion into containers
9. **Batch size:** 2-3 lbs ground beef = 4-6 meals

### How to Prep: Salmon

**Method: Oven Baked**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Place salmon fillets on parchment-lined sheet pan
3. Season with salt, pepper, lemon, and herbs
4. Drizzle with avocado oil
5. Bake 12-15 minutes until flaky
6. Store in glass containers (lasts 3-4 days)
7. **Batch size:** 4 fillets = 2-4 meals

### How to Prep: Hard-Boiled Eggs

**Method: Instant Pot or Stovetop**
1. Place eggs in Instant Pot (or pot of water)
2. Instant Pot: High pressure 5 min, quick release
3. Stovetop: Boil 10 minutes, then ice bath
4. Peel and store in containers
5. **Storage:** Up to 7 days in fridge
6. **Batch size:** 1 dozen eggs = 12 quick breakfasts

## Vegetable Prep

### Best Vegetables for Meal Prep

**Roasting (Lasts 5-7 days)**
- Broccoli
- Cauliflower
- Brussels sprouts
- Zucchini
- Bell peppers
- Asparagus
- Carrots
- Sweet potatoes

**Raw/Minimal Prep (Lasts 3-5 days)**
- Spinach
- Kale
- Mixed greens
- Cucumbers
- Cherry tomatoes
- Celery
- Carrots (raw or cooked)

### How to Prep: Roasted Vegetables

**Method: Sheet Pan Roasting**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Chop vegetables into similar-sized pieces (1-2 inches)
3. Toss with ghee/avocado oil (2-3 tablespoons per sheet pan)
4. Season generously with salt and pepper
5. Optional: add garlic powder, paprika, herbs
6. Spread on sheet pans in single layer
7. Roast 25-35 minutes, stirring halfway through
8. Cool completely before storing
9. Store in glass containers
10. **Batch size:** 2-3 sheet pans = 6-8 servings

### How to Prep: Fresh Vegetables

**Salad Prep**
1. Wash and dry all greens thoroughly (use salad spinner)
2. Store in glass containers lined with paper towels
3. Store dressing separately (prevents sogginess)
4. **Lasts:** 3-5 days for greens, keep dressing for up to 7 days

**Veggie Sticks for Snacking**
1. Wash and peel vegetables
2. Cut carrots, celery, cucumbers into stick form
3. Store in water-filled containers in fridge
4. **Lasts:** 5-7 days

### How to Prep: Sweet Potatoes

**Method: Oven Roasted**
1. Wash sweet potatoes thoroughly
2. Poke with fork multiple times
3. Wrap in foil (optional)
4. Bake at 400°F for 45-60 minutes until soft
5. Cool, peel, and portion
6. Store in containers
7. **Batch size:** 4-5 potatoes = 4-8 meals
8. **Reheating:** Microwave 2-3 minutes or add to skillet

## Healthy Fat Prep

### Pre-Portioned Healthy Fats

**Ghee/Coconut Oil Portions**
- Spoon into ice cube trays
- Freeze overnight
- Pop out and store in container
- Use 1-2 cubes per meal
- **Each cube ≈ 1 tablespoon**

**Avocado Prep**
- Buy ripe avocados mid-week
- Store whole in fridge
- Cut fresh each day (prevents browning)
- Alternative: squeeze lemon juice, store in container for 1-2 days

**Nut Butter Portioning**
- Pre-portion into small containers
- 2 tablespoons per snack portion
- Store at room temperature
- **Lasts:** 2+ weeks

**Olive Oil/Avocado Oil**
- Store in cool, dark place
- Use within 2 months of opening
- Keep in portion-controlled bottle

## Storage Tips

### Container Strategy
- **Glass containers with lids** are best (see-through, lasts forever)
- Use size-appropriate containers (prevents oxidation)
- Label with contents and date

### Organization System
```
PROTEINS (Front Left)
- Cooked chicken (3-4 containers)
- Cooked ground beef (2-3 containers)
- Hard-boiled eggs (1 container)

VEGETABLES (Front Right)
- Roasted broccoli
- Roasted sweet potato
- Roasted bell peppers
- Fresh salad greens
- Raw veggie sticks

COMPLIANT SAUCES (Top Shelf)
- Olive oil
- Ghee portions
- Nut butters
- Dressings

SNACKS (Separate Shelf)
- Nuts
- Fruit
- Jerky
- Olives
```

### Freezer Organization
- **Freeze proteins** if prepping for more than 4-5 days
- Use freezer bags or containers
- Label clearly with type and date
- **Thaw method:** Move to fridge night before use

## Make-Ahead Recipes

### Whole30 Compliant Marinara Sauce (Freezes Well!)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon dried basil

**Instructions:**
1. Heat olive oil in large pot
2. Sauté garlic 1 minute
3. Add tomatoes and seasonings
4. Simmer 20 minutes, stirring occasionally
5. Cool and portion into containers
6. Freeze in portions (1-2 cups each)
7. **Storage:** 7 days fridge, 3 months freezer

### Whole30 Compliant Salad Dressing

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Whisk all ingredients together
2. Store in glass jar with tight lid
3. Shake before each use
4. **Storage:** 2 weeks fridge

### Whole30 Compliant Steak Seasoning Mix

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tablespoons sea salt
- 1 tablespoon black pepper
- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Mix all spices in small jar
2. Store in pantry
3. Use 1-2 teaspoons per pound of meat
4. **Storage:** 6 months

### Make-Ahead Breakfast Scramble

**Ingredients:**
- 12 eggs, beaten
- 2 cups broccoli, chopped
- 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
- 1/2 cup bell peppers, diced
- 3 tablespoons ghee
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Heat ghee in large skillet over medium heat
2. Sauté vegetables 5 minutes until softened
3. Add beaten eggs, scramble until cooked through (8-10 minutes)
4. Season with salt and pepper
5. Cool and portion into 4 containers
6. **Storage:** 3-4 days fridge, 1 month freezer
7. **Reheating:** Microwave 2-3 minutes with splash of water

### Batch-Cooked Chili (Freezer Staple!)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 lbs ground beef
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 2 cans (14.5 oz each) diced tomatoes
- 2 cups beef broth
- 2 tablespoons chili powder
- 1 tablespoon cumin
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Brown ground beef in large pot, drain excess fat
2. Add onion, garlic, pepper; sauté 5 minutes
3. Add remaining ingredients
4. Simmer 30-40 minutes until flavors meld
5. Cool and portion into 6 containers
6. Freeze for later use
7. **Storage:** 5 days fridge, 3 months freezer
8. **Yield:** 6 servings

## Time-Saving Hacks

### Sunday Strategy (2-3 hours total)

**Hour 1:**
- [ ] Oven on: Start baking 2 proteins (chicken + salmon OR ground beef)
- [ ] Slow cooker: Start beef chili or stew
- [ ] Chop all vegetables while proteins cook

**Hour 2:**
- [ ] Proteins done: Cool and portion
- [ ] Sheet pans in: Roast 2-3 vegetable types
- [ ] Start boiling water for sweet potatoes
- [ ] Chop and prep salad vegetables

**Hour 3:**
- [ ] Cool and store everything
- [ ] Prepare a few quick snack portions
- [ ] Clean up as you go

### 30-Minute Express Meal Prep (For Busy Weeks)

If 2-3 hours isn't possible, focus on these essentials:

1. **Proteins Only** (20 min)
   - Bake chicken breasts OR
   - Brown ground beef OR
   - Cook in Instant Pot

2. **One Vegetable** (10 min)
   - Roast broccoli OR
   - Roast sweet potatoes

3. **Eggs** (10 min)
   - Hard boil a batch

**Result:** Mix & match these 3 components with fresh vegetables throughout the week

### One-Pan Wonders (Cook It All Together)

**Sheet Pan Dinner:**
1. Arrange protein on one sheet pan
2. Arrange vegetables on same pan around protein
3. Drizzle with ghee, season everything
4. Bake at 425°F for 25-35 minutes
5. One pan, zero waste, minimal cleanup

### Leftover Transformation Ideas

**Monday's Roasted Chicken → Friday's Different Meals:**
- Monday: Plain grilled chicken + roasted vegetables
- Tuesday: Shredded in salad with olive oil dressing
- Wednesday: In egg scramble with peppers
- Thursday: Mixed with avocado mayo on salad
- Friday: Shredded in chili or soup

## Shopping Strategy for Meal Prep Success

### Buy in Bulk
- Chicken: 2-3 breasts per meal day you're prepping
- Ground beef: 2-3 lbs for variety
- Eggs: 2-3 dozen for the month
- Vegetables: Mix of fresh and frozen

### Quality Over Quantity
- Choose **grass-fed beef** when possible
- Select **pasture-raised eggs**
- Buy **wild-caught fish**
- Prefer **organic produce** for thin-skinned items

### Budget Tips
- Buy proteins on sale, freeze immediately
- Buy seasonal vegetables (cheaper + fresher)
- Buy frozen vegetables for roasting (saves prep time)
- Buy store brand spices (huge savings)

## Meal Prep Troubleshooting

**Q: My prepped food tastes bland by Wednesday**
A: Prep components separately, not complete meals. Assemble fresh each day with different seasonings and sauces.

**Q: Proteins dry out in the fridge**
A: Store in shallow containers with minimal air space. Consider storing with a bit of cooking liquid or broth.

**Q: I get bored with the same meals**
A: Prep 2-3 proteins and 3-4 vegetables. Mix combinations throughout the week = 6-12 different meals.

**Q: I don't have time for 3-hour prep**
A: Do express prep (30 min) + cook dinners fresh 3-4 times per week. Less efficient but still helpful.

**Q: Can I meal prep breakfast?**
A: Yes! Make egg scrambles, frittatas, or breakfast sausage. Reheat in morning.

**Q: How do I prevent food waste?**
A: Only prep what you'll actually eat. Start with 3-4 days worth, not a full week.
